My Experience

My first training was in physical health and that was when I discovered the power of healing. I witnessed people thriving not because they were given medications or went under procedures. They healed because they were offered emotional support and understanding of what they were going through. This moment of realization made me to choose a career in a psychology in psychotherapy.

Over the course of nearly 30 years, I worked in inpatient settings, NHS urgent, community and specialist services, voluntary sectors, Local Authorities, schools and as a freelance. I offered an individual therapy to children and adults, a group and couple therapy.

My clinical work has brought me into contact with a broad spectrum of mental health presentations, including but not limited to personality disorders, mood disorders, psychosis, eating disorders, suicidality, self — harm, dual diagnoses, neurodiversity, learning difficulties and complex post — traumatic stress disorder (CPTSD).

Over the years, I have cultivated expertise in several therapeutic modalities, including Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behaviour Therapy (DBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), eye movement desensitization and reprocessing (EMDR), art and dramatherapy, attachment-based therapy, and existential approaches. My interventions are client — centred and often integrate verbal and nonverbal communication strategies to address the unique needs of each service user.

My eclectic approach emphasises fostering safety, skills development, and mentalization as key components of brief psychological interventions. Creative therapy, underpinned by a DBT framework, has been instrumental in teaching distress tolerance and emotional regulation skills, particularly for individuals with personality disorders and developmental trauma.

Creative Therapies

In the realm of psychotherapy, words alone don’t always capture the depth of human emotions and experiences. Sometimes, healing requires a different language — one that transcends verbal expression. That’s where arts-based, drama, and body — centred therapies come in. These creative and somatic approaches offer powerful ways to access, process, and heal psychological wounds, helping clients reconnect with themselves in profound ways.

Why Go Beyond Talk Therapy?

Traditional talk therapy is effective, but for many, it’s not the full picture. Trauma, stress, and emotional pain are often stored not just in the mind but also in the body. Some emotions or experiences may also be difficult to articulate through words alone. Arts — based, drama, and somatic (body — focused) therapies engage multiple senses and creative channels, allowing for a deeper and more holistic healing process.

  • Encourages self — expression – Art provides a nonverbal way to express deep emotions.
  • Reduces anxiety and stress – The creative process itself can be calming and meditative.
  • Accesses subconscious thoughts – Artistic expression can reveal feelings that may be hard to articulate.
  • Enhances self — awareness – Visual representation of emotions can lead to greater insight.

The Power of Dramatherapy

Drama therapy incorporates role — playing, storytelling, and movement to help clients explore emotions and experiences in a safe and supportive environment.

  • Explores different perspectives – Clients can step into different roles, gaining new insights.
  • Releases suppressed emotions – Acting out experiences can help process trauma or unresolved conflicts.
  • Builds confidence and resilience – Dramatic expression fosters self — discovery and empowerment.
  • Strengthens social skills – Improves communication, empathy, and relationship dynamics.

The Role of the Body in Psychotherapy

Our bodies store emotions, and movement — based therapies help release feelings that might not emerge through talk alone. Somatic therapy, dance therapy, and breathwork support clients in reconnecting with their bodies and regulating their nervous systems.

  • Releases stored trauma – Movement can process trauma “frozen” in the body.
  • Regulates the nervous system – Practices like deep breathing and mindful movement promote relaxation.
  • Restores mind — body connection – Clients learn to trust and listen to their bodies.
  • Builds emotional resilience – Physical expression helps process and integrate emotions.

Who Can Benefit from Creative and Body — Based Therapies?

These approaches are especially beneficial for individuals experiencing:

  • Trauma and PTSD
  • Anxiety and depression
  • Stress and burnout
  • Grief and loss
  • Low self — esteem
  • Relationship challenges
  • Emotional blocks

Talking Therapies

A Holistic Approach to Healing: EMDR, ACT, DBT, Attachment — Based, and Existential Therapy.

At my practice, I integrate evidence — based and experiential therapeutic approaches to support individuals in navigating their emotional challenges, healing past wounds, and building meaningful lives. Every person’s journey is unique, and therapy should reflect that. That’s why I offer a blend of Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), Dialectical Behaviour Therapy (DBT), attachment — based therapy, and existential therapy — each tailored to your specific needs.

EMDR: Processing Trauma and Unblocking Healing

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR) is a highly effective therapy for individuals dealing with trauma, PTSD, anxiety, and distressing memories. This structured approach helps the brain reprocess painful experiences, reducing their emotional intensity.

  • Releases distress from past experiences without requiring extensive verbal recounting.
  • Reduces PTSD symptoms and emotional reactivity linked to traumatic memories.
  • Helps break patterns of fear, avoidance, and self — limiting beliefs.
  • Strengthens resilience and allows for a healthier emotional response to triggers.

ACT: Living with Purpose and Psychological Flexibility

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) is a mindfulness — based approach that helps individuals accept their thoughts and emotions rather than struggling against them. It focuses on taking meaningful action in alignment with personal values.

  • Encourages acceptance of difficult emotions rather than avoidance.
  • Promotes psychological flexibility, reducing the impact of negative thoughts.
  • Helps build a meaningful life by identifying and acting on core values.
  • Supports individuals with anxiety, depression, chronic pain, and trauma.

DBT: Emotional Regulation and Interpersonal Growth

Dialectical Behavioural Therapy (DBT) is particularly beneficial for individuals struggling with emotional dysregulation, intense relationships, or self — destructive behaviours. Originally developed for borderline personality disorder, it is now widely used for a range of emotional challenges.

  • Teaches emotion regulation strategies to manage overwhelming feelings.
  • Enhances distress tolerance, reducing impulsive reactions.
  • Improves interpersonal effectiveness, fostering healthier relationships.
  • Encourages mindfulness, helping individuals stay present and grounded.

Attachment — Based Therapy: Healing Relationship Patterns

Our early relationships shape how we connect with others throughout life. Attachment — based therapy focuses on understanding these patterns and creating secure, healthy connections.

  • Identifies and heals relational wounds from childhood or past experiences.
  • Improves self-esteem and self — worth by addressing unmet emotional needs.
  • Enhances relationships by fostering secure attachment and communication skills.
  • Supports individuals with anxiety, depression, trauma, and relational difficulties.
